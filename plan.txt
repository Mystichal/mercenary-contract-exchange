💰 5. How contracts behave like market assets

Contracts gain price dynamics based on risk and probability of success.

They can be:
	Bought
	Mercenary accepts mission.
	Resold
	Holder transfers execution rights.
	Fractionalized
	Multiple operators complete different phases.
	Hedged
	Insurance against failure.
	Speculated on
	Buy underpriced contracts → subcontract cheaper.


📈 6. Market Structure

You can support multiple trading models.
	Fixed-price acceptance
	Issuer sets reward. First taker wins.
	Simple bounty board.
	Auction model
	Mercenaries bid down price they are willing to accept.
	Issuer gets best rate.
	Tradable contract rights (powerful)
	Contract is tokenized and transferable.

Example:
	Tribe offers 10,000 for escort mission
	Mercenary accepts
	Later sells contract to someone else for 8,000
	Profit = 2,000 margin
	This creates a secondary market.
	Risk-adjusted pricing market
	Contracts listed with expected probability of completion.
	Market prices execution difficulty.
	This is basically a derivatives market for missions.


🧾 7. Escrow and Settlement Model

Payment must be locked before execution.

Escrow holds:
	resource reward
	issuer collateral
	executor bond (optional)

Settlement rules:
	if success → pay executor
	if failure → return or slash collateral
	if dispute → arbitration logic


Everything enforced automatically.
